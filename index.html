  • The primary complication: mucus plugging
  • Much of tracheostomy care occurs in the home, without a nurse.
  • Home caregivers are exhausted, and don’t catch mucus plugs until it’s too late.

The Need

A way to monitor mucus buildup in a pediatric tracheostomy tube and alert a caregiver so that an active pediatric patient’s airway may be reestablished before complete tube obstruction and resulting suffocation.


Our Solution

  • Attaches externally to a tracheotomy tube
  • Direct detection of obstruction build-up
  • Signals caregiver when there is an obstruction
  • Small and lightweight for free movement of user

Prototype and Testing

  • Distinguishes distinct levels of obstruction based on flow rate provided by flow sensor
  • Sends medium and high alerts via alarm for partial and full obstructions, respectively

Future Work

 Frequency analysis for more robust monitoring and prompt alarms
Wireless data transmission to increase mobility of the device
Product development, investigation of commercialization potential via EIH Summer Incubator
